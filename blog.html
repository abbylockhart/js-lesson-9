Gold Coast and Nearby Waterfalls and Swimming Holes

Killen Falls, Tintenbar

Killen Falls is a beautiful spot near Tintenbar with a picturesque waterfall and a serene swimming hole. The trail to the falls is relatively short and easy, making it accessible for most people.

The estimated water temperature at Killen Falls is around 20°C, which is refreshing but not too cold. The area around the falls is lush and green, providing a perfect backdrop for a relaxing day out.

It takes approximately 10 minutes to walk the 660-meter trail to the falls. The trail is well-maintained and easy to follow. Swimming conditions are generally safe, but always exercise caution and check for any warnings or advisories before taking a dip.

Cedar Creek Falls, Mount Tamborine

Cedar Creek Falls is located in Mount Tamborine and offers a series of cascades and rock pools perfect for swimming. The falls are surrounded by lush rainforest, making it a picturesque and tranquil spot.

The estimated water temperature at Cedar Creek Falls is around 18°C. The cooler water can be quite refreshing, especially on a hot day. The trail to the falls is moderately difficult, with some steep sections and uneven terrain.

The trail to Cedar Creek Falls is about 1.1 km long and takes around 20-30 minutes to walk. The swimming conditions are generally safe, but always be mindful of the water flow and depth, as they can change with weather conditions.

Buderim Falls, Buderim

Buderim Falls, also known as Serenity Falls, is a tranquil spot in Buderim with a lovely waterfall and lush surroundings. The area is well-shaded by the dense forest, providing a cool escape from the heat.

The estimated water temperature at Buderim Falls is around 19°C. The water is clear and inviting, perfect for a refreshing swim. The trail to the falls is relatively short and easy, suitable for families and casual hikers.

The trail to Buderim Falls is about 600 meters long and takes around 10-15 minutes to walk. The path is well-maintained and easy to navigate. Swimming conditions are usually safe, but always be cautious and observe any posted signs or warnings.
